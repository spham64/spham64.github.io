Info

What

Gunpla (Gundam plastic model) are plastic model kits based on the popular anime franchise, Mobile Suit Gundam. Gunpla models depict a variety of mecha from the Gundam multiverse. The model kits come in various grades which determine the size, complexity, and articulation of the models. Gunpla is a great hobby for those who enjoy building and customizing models. It encourages creativity as many builders often paint and modify their kits to their liking.

Recommended Tools

  • Nippers (Side Cutters)
  • Hobby Knife (X-Acto or Precision Cutter)
  • Tweezers
  • Sanding Sticks/Sponge Files
  • Panel Lining Pens/Washes

When

Gunpla kits can be built at any time of the day. The time required to complete a kit depends on its grade, complexity, and level of detail thus many builders choose to build it over multiple days, weeks, or even months! Many builders will choose build during the evening or on weekends as they can fully relax. However, Gunpla is about enjoyment so the best time to build is whenever you want to!

How

Step-by-step Instructions

  1. Unbox & Organize – Open the kit, check the instruction manual, and sort the plastic runners.
  2. Cut Out Parts – Use nippers to carefully remove pieces from the sprue (plastic frame).
  3. Clean Up – Trim excess plastic (nub marks) with a hobby knife or sanding sticks.
  4. Assemble the Model – Follow the manual, snapping pieces together (no glue needed).
  5. Panel Lining & Detailing (Optional) – Use panel lining pens or washes to enhance details.
  6. Apply Stickers/Decals (Optional) – Add markings for a more realistic look.
  7. Top Coat & Customization (Optional) – Spray a matte, gloss, or semi-gloss coat for protection and finish.
  8. Final Touches & Display – Pose the model and place it in a stand or diorama.
